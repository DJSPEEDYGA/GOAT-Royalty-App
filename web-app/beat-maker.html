Welcome to the GOAT Beat Machine
GOAT Force Records
FL Studio inspired step-sequencer, piano roll, playlist and mixer — all in one self-contained web app. Build trap, boom-bap, house, R&B and lofi beats directly in your browser.
Quick Start
1. Click any step button in the Channel Rack to add a hit.
2. Press Space to play / stop the beat.
3. Switch to F6 Channel Rack, F7 Piano Roll, or F9 Mixer.
4. Use the AI Beat button to generate a full trap pattern instantly.
5. Export your beat to WAV or save it to localStorage.
Channel Rack
The left side shows channel names, mute (M), solo (S) and FX send buttons. The knobs control volume and pan per channel. Click the channel name twice to rename. Right-click a step for velocity and probability options.
Piano Roll
Draw notes by clicking the grid. Drag notes to move them. Right-click a note to delete. Use the Quantize button to snap notes to the grid. Select a melody channel to compose bass lines or leads.
Playlist
Arrange patterns across bars on the timeline. Click a track lane to place the current pattern. Right-click a placed block to remove it. The timeline shows 64 bars of arrangement room.
Mixer
Each channel strip has a volume fader, pan knob, level meter and FX send selector. The master strip on the right controls the final output. Click the FX slot to route a channel to reverb, delay, distortion, chorus, EQ or compression.
Keyboard Shortcuts
Space Play/Stop   F5 Playlist   F6 Rack   F7 Piano   F9 Mixer   CtrlZ Undo   CtrlS Save   1–9 Pattern select
Audio Engine
The synthesizer uses the Web Audio API to generate kick, snare, clap, hi-hat, open hat, tom, percussion, 808 bass, and two melody channels. All synthesis is done in real time — no samples required. A master limiter keeps levels safe.
Production Tips
Trap: Layer the kick on every downbeat and add hi-hat rolls on the off-beats. Use the 808 bass to outline the root notes of your progression. Keep the BPM between 130 and 150 for a modern trap feel.

Boom Bap: Program a snare or clap on beat 2 and 4. Add a swung hi-hat pattern and a sub-bass that follows the sample. Lower the BPM to 85–100 for that classic head-nod groove.

House: Use a four-on-the-floor kick and open hi-hat on the offbeats. Add a sidechain-style pumping effect by keeping the bass simple and the kick prominent. 120–130 BPM is the sweet spot.

R&B: Space out the drums and let the melody breathe. Use soft kicks, snares on the backbeat, and sparse hi-hats. Melody channels should play smooth chords or arpeggios.

Lofi: Layer the drums with swing and humanize velocities. Use the melody channels for jazzy chords and the 808 for a warm, subby bass. Keep it relaxed at 70–90 BPM.
